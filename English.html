AntConc

AntConc is a concordancer. It is a tool that allows to analyze corpora.

Despite its many advantages, it also has some disadvantages which are summarized below.

The multilingual dimension is not handled. The issue with AntConc is that it only performs a morphological analysis.

There is no morpho-syntactic analysis, which is typical of most concordancers. This can be a limitation in terms of multilingual management.

As we found with the Ewe version of the term "redemption", the spelling of the term did not allow AntConc to find it and we had to use another method to find the equivalent term.

Sometimes, when launching certain searches, we had to wait a long time; this proves that the management of spelling and complex queries by AntConc is not ideal, which is the opposite of a tool such as Unitex Gramalab which allows to manage the spelling of the words.

In English, there is already a difference: "blood" appears in 16th position and two times; "jesus" in 42nd position once and "jerusalem", in 43rd position once.

In Spanish, "sangre" (blood) appears in 10th position for a frequency of two times; "jesús" in 43rd position for a frequency of one time followed by "jerusalén" in 44th position, also for a frequency of one time.
